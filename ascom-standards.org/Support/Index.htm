How to Get Support

If your device (e.g., telescope) shipped with its ASCOM driver, contact the device manufacturer first. Otherwise, if your device has a driver that was developed by a third party developer, the first place to get help is directly from the driver developer. The people to whom you paid the money for your program or device are always the best place to get help because they know the most about your program or device.

If you can't get help from the people responsible for developing your program or device and driver, you can try getting help on the ASCOM Talk group. People on the ASCOM-Talk group are happy to help with problems, but keep in mind that they generally won't know about the internals of your device. Most driver authors want people to use their drivers and will at least lurk on the group. There are a number of things that you can do to help these people to help you.

  1. Report information accurately and completely. It is almost impossible to help if insufficient information is given. We get error reports that basically say "ASCOM isn't working, please fix it". ASCOM is neither an application, nor a driver, nor a device. This sort of request for help is unanswerable and will result in disappointment and frustration for you.
  2. Read the help that comes with the app or device. Many support questions have already been answered in the supplied help. This should be accessible from the app's help menu, the driver setup, or its properties window.
  3. If you can cause the problem at will, write down the steps you took to cause it, producing a "repro scenario". This can be invaluable information.
  4. Contact the app or driver author or a supporter directly or on the ASCOM-Talk group. Look in the app or driver help file for contact info.
Uninstalling and reinstalling the ASCOM Platform, and/or a program/application you are struggling with, is almost always a waste of time. The problems we see are most commonly caused by flaky cables, driver bugs, bad USB connectors, bad power supplies for devices, or other hardware and driver related issues (see below). What the device maker, driver author and people here who try to help need most is a way to make the problem happen reproduceably. There are no gremlins living in computers and devices. The causes of problems can virtually always be explained logically. Give the developers and others who want to help a chance to zero in on the problem and solve it. Keep in mind that "ASCOM" and the "ASCOM Platform" is a set of documentation, tools, and libraries used by many developers. It is not a "program" that is causing your problem.

Remember that we aren't looking over your shoulder; we can't see things that are totally obvious to you. We need to know:

  • What brand of device (e.g. telescope and mount) you are using.
  • What driver you have selected, including its version (normally visible on the driver setup or properties window).
  • What application you are using.
  • What operating system you are using, including the release number etc..
  • Exactly what you did, what worked and what did not.
  • An exact reproduction of any error messages, with what was done just before the error happened.
  • Ideally an exact sequence of actions that will generate the error ( repro scenario).

Things to Try First for Devices

If a device or driver doesn't work at all then in the majority of cases it is one of the following causes:

  • The connection cable between the PC and the mount.
  • The cable between the PC and the mount (it's worth double checking, this is the source of the majority of problems).
  • The USB to serial connector. These are very variable, good ones are fine but there are a lot that aren't so good and it's not obvious which are which.
  • Is the right cable being used? (The Meade cable doesn't work with the Celestron and (presumably) vice versa).
  • Is the cable connected to the correct port on the scope?
  • Is the correct COM port being used? Some (poorly designed) USB to serial connectors can select a different port each time they are connected, especially if they are plugged into a different USB socket or in a different order.
  • Is the correct driver being used?
  • Installation problems tend to give rather cryptic errors saying that something does not exist or cannot be loaded. If you get these right at the start of trying to connect, or especially if you get this when you are trying to select a driver, then try reinstalling the ASCOM platform, then the driver. It may be necessary to uninstall the current installation and reinstall it.

If the driver connects and some things work but some don't or the driver connects in one application but not in others then the basic connectivity is OK and the challenge is to find out why a particular function isn't working; we need to know:

  • What works, let us know what programs or functions work for you.
  • What doesn't work, which program and where in the program it fails.
  • The exact way it fails, including what you do, what happens and exactly what error messages are shown.
  • If you are only trying advanced functions, such as guiding, but haven't tried something simple then it is a good idea to try a simple planetarium program, such as Cartes du Ciel, and see if the basic connectivity and control works.

A good technique, especially for application or script authors, is to post a code fragment that demonstrates the problem. Try to reduce this to the minimum that shows the problem.

Remember that some of the people involved in ASCOM are volunteers and often have other jobs, so replies can take a few days.
